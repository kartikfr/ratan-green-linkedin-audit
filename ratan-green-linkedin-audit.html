01

Where Growth Is Leaking

Six issues holding back a strong existing page

High Priority

Strong Project Data Is Buried Inside Long Captions

Recent project posts include excellent evidence: 150 kW, 300 kW and 560 kW installations, projected annual generation, financial value, and CO₂ reduction. But the business outcome sits deep inside caption text instead of becoming the visual headline buyers remember.

Fix

Turn every project into a five-slide proof story: client challenge, system size, annual generation, financial outcome, and a direct enquiry CTA. Lead with the result, not the commissioning announcement.

High Priority

The Tag and Hashtag Blocks Reduce Clarity

Many posts end with a long list of employees, partners, and 10–15 broad hashtags. This makes the strongest proof feel promotional and obscures the action a buyer, vendor, or future employee should take next.

Fix

Use 3–5 precise hashtags, tag only people directly involved in the story, and reserve partner mentions for a short acknowledgement line. Give every post one clear audience and one clear action.

High Priority

Good Captions Still Need a Buyer-Led Hook

The page communicates technical detail, but several posts begin with ceremonial or announcement language. The strongest vendor post works because it immediately names the need, geography, project type, and response route. Project and educational posts should be just as specific.

Caption structure every post needs
1
Hook: one line that stops the scroll
2
Context: what's happening and why it matters to the reader
3
Insight: a number, a position, or a lesson they can act on
4
CTA: matched to content stage: question for education, DM for leads
Critical

Leadership Is Tagged, Not Yet Leading the Conversation

Ajay Singh Shekhawat and senior team members are regularly tagged, and leadership appointments generate strong reactions. But the recent company feed does not consistently turn their operating experience into first-person views on procurement, policy, project risk, or industrial solar economics.

Fix

Publish two founder-led posts per month: one market position and one field lesson. Cross-post from Ajay's profile and the company page, with an employee-comment plan in the first hour.

Critical

Conversion Is Inconsistent and Hard to Measure

The vendor-recruitment post provides email, phone, and DM routes, while most project and educational posts stop at awareness. There is no visible campaign-specific link or consistent mechanism to distinguish buyer interest from general engagement.

Fix

Create three CTA paths: project consultation, vendor partnership, and careers. Use one tracked landing link or DM keyword per campaign and report enquiries, qualified conversations, and meetings—not reactions alone.

High Priority

Recent Content Relies Heavily on Static Images

The reviewed public sample shows project galleries and designed occasion posts, but little visible use of native video, document carousels, or engineer-led explainers. That leaves valuable site access and team expertise underused.

Fix

Each month: two document carousels, one 45–60 second site walkthrough, and one engineer explainer. Film on site, subtitle for silent viewing, and open with the business result in the first two seconds.
